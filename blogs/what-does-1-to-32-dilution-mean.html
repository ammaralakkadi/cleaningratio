What Does a 1:32 Dilution Mean? Exact mL + oz for 500 mL, 1 L, and 32 oz Bottles

1:32 shows up on cleaner labels all the time. The trick is translating it into your exact bottle size without guessing.

9–11 min read • Cleaning dilution ratios

Part of the main guide

This article belongs to the Cleaning Dilution Guide, where readers can move between ratio basics, label wording, bucket scaling, and bottle calculations.

Quick answer

A 1:32 dilution means 1 part concentrate mixed with 32 parts water. If you’re filling a bottle to a final volume, that works out to roughly 3% concentrate in the finished mix. To avoid math mistakes (especially on odd bottle sizes), scale it with the Cleaning Dilution Calculator. If your product label gives specific directions, follow the product label instructions.

“Parts” are flexible — your bottle size decides what a part is

“1 part” doesn’t mean a specific unit. It can be mL, oz, cups — anything — as long as you keep the same unit for everything. What matters is the ratio stays consistent.

If you want to be confident you’re reading the label correctly, this helps: How to Read Cleaning Dilution Labels.

Exact 1:32 amounts for common bottles

These examples assume you’re making a full bottle of finished solution. Measure concentrate first, then add water up to the final volume.

500 mL spray bottle

  • Concentrate: ~15 mL
  • Water: fill to 500 mL (about 485 mL water)

1 L bottle

  • Concentrate: ~30 mL
  • Water: fill to 1 L (about 970 mL water)

32 oz spray bottle

  • Concentrate: ~1.0 oz (about 30 mL)
  • Water: fill to 32 oz

If your bottle is 24 oz, 28 oz, 650 mL, or anything else, don’t eyeball it. Use the Cleaning Dilution Calculator and you’ll be done in seconds.

Why people mess up 1:32 (and how to avoid it)

Most mistakes come from mixing up two different ideas:

  • “1:32” as concentrate:water (this is what labels usually mean)
  • “1:32” as concentrate:final solution (different math, different result)

If you want the clean explanation with examples, read: How to Calculate Dilution Ratio.

Quick comparison: 1:32 vs 1:64

1:64 is simply weaker than 1:32 (half as much concentrate per amount of water). If your label mentions 1:64 on other tasks, this post helps you keep them straight: What Does a 1:64 Dilution Mean?.

Bottom line

1:32 means 1 part concentrate to 32 parts water. For most people, the easiest way to get it right on any bottle size is to use the Cleaning Dilution Calculator. When your product label provides dilution directions, follow the product label instructions.
