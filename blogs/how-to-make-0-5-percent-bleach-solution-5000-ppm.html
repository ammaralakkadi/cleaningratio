How to Make a 0.5% Bleach Solution (5000 ppm): Exact Mixing for Spray Bottles + Buckets

0.5% is a common target people search for. The only reliable way to mix it is label-first: find your bleach %, then measure exactly.

12–14 min read • Bleach dilution

Part of the main guide

This article belongs to the Bleach Dilution Guide, where readers can move between bleach ppm targets, dilution basics, label-first mixing, and related bleach safety articles.

Quick answer

A 0.5% bleach solution is roughly 5000 ppm available chlorine. To make it correctly, check the starting bleach percentage on your product label (commonly 5–8.25%), then use the Bleach Dilution Calculator to get an exact measured amount for your bottle or bucket. If your product label provides dilution directions for your intended use, follow the product label instructions.

Start with the label % (don’t guess the strength)

“Bleach” isn’t one fixed concentration. Two bottles can look similar and be different strengths. If you assume the wrong %, you can land too strong (more fumes, more surface damage risk) or too weak for your target.

If you want the fastest way to avoid the usual mistakes, read: Bleach Dilution Mistakes to Avoid.

When 0.5% makes sense (and when it doesn’t)

People often reach for a stronger mix when a milder one would do. If you’re cleaning routine household mess, 0.5% can be overkill. For many situations, a lower target is more practical and easier on surfaces.

If you’re trying to match a specific guideline or product direction, stick to that and follow the product label instructions. This page is about measuring a 0.5% target safely — not recommending where you should use it.

The clean way to mix: calculator → measure → add water

Use the Bleach Dilution Calculator with:

  • Your bleach % from the label
  • Target: 0.5%
  • Your container size (500 mL, 1 L, 32 oz, 5 gallons — anything)

Measure bleach into the container first, then add water up to the final volume. This keeps the numbers clean and repeatable.

Practical examples (common label strengths)

These examples are here so you can sanity-check your results. If your bleach % is different, don’t “round it” by feel — use the calculator and measure accurately. And again: follow the product label instructions.

0.5% from 5% bleach

  • 500 mL bottle: 50 mL bleach + 450 mL water
  • 1 L bottle: 100 mL bleach + 900 mL water
  • 32 oz bottle: ~95 mL bleach + water to 32 oz
  • 5-gallon bucket: ~1.89 L bleach + water to 5 gallons (most people will prefer smaller batches; this is a lot)

0.5% from 8.25% bleach

  • 500 mL bottle: ~30 mL bleach + water to 500 mL
  • 1 L bottle: ~61 mL bleach + water to 1 L
  • 32 oz bottle: ~58 mL bleach + water to 32 oz
  • 5-gallon bucket: ~1.15 L bleach + water to 5 gallons

Want exact amounts for your specific bottle and label strength? Use the Bleach Dilution Calculator.

Safety notes that matter for stronger mixes

  • Never mix bleach with other cleaners. Don’t combine with acids (like vinegar) or ammonia products.
  • Ventilation matters. If fumes feel strong, stop and increase airflow.
  • Use cool water. Hot water can increase odor and speed breakdown.
  • Keep contact with metals minimal unless the product label allows it.
  • Follow the product label instructions for surfaces, contact time, and handling.

Make it fresh (diluted bleach doesn’t stay “the same”)

Diluted bleach loses potency over time — especially with light and heat. If strength matters for what you’re doing, read: How Long Does Diluted Bleach Last?.

If your real-world use is a bucket workflow, you’ll also like: How Much Bleach for a 5-Gallon Bucket?.

Bottom line

0.5% (about 5000 ppm) is simple to mix when you do it label-first: read your bleach %, then measure using the Bleach Dilution Calculator. Don't eyeball it — if your target is in ppm, the PPM Dilution Calculator handles that directly. When the label gives instructions for your specific use, follow the product label instructions.
