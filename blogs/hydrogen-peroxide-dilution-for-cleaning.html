Hydrogen Peroxide Dilution for Cleaning (3% to 1%): Practical Ratios + Exact Amounts

Hydrogen peroxide can be useful for some cleaning jobs, but it works best when you keep it simple: use the right strength, don’t mix it with other cleaners, and measure your dilution once so you can repeat it.

11 min read • Peroxide dilution

Part of the main guide

This article belongs to the Surface Cleaning Guide, where readers can compare hydrogen peroxide with alcohol, dish soap, Pine-Sol, Fabuloso, and other practical surface-cleaning dilution guides.

Quick answer

If you have regular store-bought 3% hydrogen peroxide and you want a milder mix like 1%, the dilution is simple: 1 part peroxide + 2 parts water. For 0.5%, it’s 1 part peroxide + 5 parts water. To get exact amounts for any bottle size (no guessing), use the Cleaning Dilution Calculator.

Safety baseline: follow product label instructions. Don’t mix peroxide with other cleaners (especially vinegar or bleach). When in doubt, don’t mix.

What to use (and what to avoid)

This guide assumes you’re starting with standard 3% hydrogen peroxide sold for household use. If you have a high-concentration product (often marketed as “food grade” peroxide), don’t try to DIY dilutions at home. It’s easier and safer to buy standard 3% instead.

Peroxide can discolor fabrics and affect some finishes. If the surface matters, test a small hidden area first.

Exact amounts (3% → 1% and 3% → 0.5%)

These are measured amounts for common containers. Small rounding is fine. If your bottle size is different, jump to the calculator method below.

Dilute 3% hydrogen peroxide to 1%

  • 500 mL final: 167 mL of 3% + 333 mL water
  • 1 liter final: 333 mL of 3% + 667 mL water
  • 32 oz final: 10.7 oz of 3% + 21.3 oz water

Ratio shortcut (3% → 1%): it’s simply 1 part peroxide : 2 parts water.

Dilute 3% hydrogen peroxide to 0.5%

  • 500 mL final: 83 mL of 3% + 417 mL water
  • 1 liter final: 167 mL of 3% + 833 mL water
  • 32 oz final: 5.3 oz of 3% + 26.7 oz water

If you’re trying to match a specific product instruction, follow the label. These ratios are general-purpose dilution math—not a replacement for label directions.

Practical use notes (simple, conservative)

  • Don’t mix cleaners: peroxide + vinegar and peroxide + bleach are both “don’t do it” combinations.
  • Use a clean bottle: rinse thoroughly before filling.
  • Store smart: peroxide breaks down with light. Keep it in an opaque/dark container if possible.
  • Spot test: if the surface finish matters, test first.

FAQs

Can I mix hydrogen peroxide with vinegar?

Don’t mix them. Keep peroxide separate from other cleaners unless a product label explicitly says it’s compatible.

Can I mix hydrogen peroxide with bleach?

Don’t mix cleaners. If you’re using bleach for a specific job, use bleach alone at the label dilution. If you want to measure bleach properly, use the Bleach Dilution Calculator.

Do I need to rinse after using peroxide?

It depends on the surface and the product instructions. For food-contact surfaces or sensitive materials, follow label guidance and rinse if required.
